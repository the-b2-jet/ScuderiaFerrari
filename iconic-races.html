UNFORGETTABLE VICTORIES

Races that will forever be in Ferrari folklore

1951

British Grand Prix

  • Driver: José Froilán González
  • Track: Silverstone Circuit
  • Ferrari's first ever F1 victory
  • Beat dominant Alfa Romeos
"The horse has bolted!" - Enzo Ferrari
1976

Italian Grand Prix

  • Driver: Niki Lauda
  • Track: Monza
  • Return after horrific crash
  • Finished 4th just 6 weeks later
"Most emotional victory" - Niki Lauda
2000

Japanese Grand Prix

  • Driver: Michael Schumacher
  • Track: Suzuka
  • First Ferrari championship in 21 years
  • Ended the drought
"We did it! We did it!" - Team Radio
2008

Brazilian Grand Prix

  • Driver: Felipe Massa
  • Track: Interlagos
  • Last Ferrari constructors' title
  • Dramatic last-corner decider
"I'm proud of my team" - Felipe Massa

RACE HIGHLIGHTS

In-depth look at Ferrari's greatest moments

1979 Italian GP

Monza • 1-2 Finish
Drivers: Scheckter & Villeneuve
Significance: Famous 1-2 for Tifosi
Quote: "Greatest day for Ferrari"
"The last Ferrari 1-2 at Monza for 21 years, securing the championship."

2003 Italian GP

Monza • Title Decider
Driver: Michael Schumacher
Significance: Equaled Fangio's record
Result: Victory & Championship
"Won at Ferrari's home track to secure his 5th world championship."

1996 Spanish GP

Catalunya • Debut Win
Driver: Michael Schumacher
Significance: First Ferrari win
Weather: Wet race masterclass
"Schumacher's first win for Ferrari in treacherous wet conditions."

1952 Italian GP

Monza • Historic 1-2-3-4
Drivers: Ascari, Taruffi, Farina, Fischer
Significance: Complete domination
Margin: Full podium sweep
"Ferrari's only 1-2-3-4 finish in Formula 1 history."

2007 Brazilian GP

Interlagos • Comeback Title
Driver: Kimi Räikkönen
Significance: Last Ferrari drivers' title
Comeback: From 17 points behind
"Kimi's incredible comeback to win the championship by 1 point."
